A research on human-phone interaction

Most of the people in the city center tend to use their phones when they move around. Some of them use it actively for calls, texts, socials, and directions; others hold it in their hand, just in case they will need it because they feel awkward or under social pressure. The phone creates an immersive bubble that muffles people’s awareness about the surrounding environment. An excessive detachment from the world around them can create potentially dangerous situations both for the phone’s users and for the people around them. Because of the phone, our body language gets misinterpreted, making people around us confused on in which direction we are going to make our next step. The design aims to create more balance between pedestrians while using their phones, by making them more aware of their surroundings and less focused on the task on the phone.

This project explores the dynamics that arise from everyday interactions between people, objects, and services. Its primary goal is to identify pain points within these interactions and design improved experiences that address those challenges. The central theme of the project is “Balance”, interpreted through either human-product/service interactions or interactions between people mediated by design. After exploring a range of contexts, I chose to focus on the common interaction of using a smartphone while walking, a behavior many of us engage in daily.

Research Process

Philosophy explored the dualism between mind and body. The Cartesian split theory shows us that the mind can be considered the software that runs on the body, which is the hardware (The mind explains behaviour while the body is the output device). Mind and body should be perfectly balanced to consent us to move and act optimally in the environment but sometimes this is not the case. Nowadays with the use of technological devices, in particular, smartphones we tend to live in two different dimensions, our minds, and thoughts are not aligned with our actions. We receive dozens and dozens of messages, calls, e-mails, pictures, and much more. We are constantly and instantly connected with people around us or even around the world. But often we lose connection with the environment and with what is happening around us

In this project, I applied and develop research and design skills iteratively. My aim was to design and improve the current interactions explained in the Concept and to experience and express the value of research in shaping and pursuing this aim. I developed my own. design-minded research style and a research-minded design style. The insights achieved during the research and design activities enabled me to develop a combined design and research approach where the insights help my design and vice versa. I applied different types of design and research methods, i.e., explorative, generative, and evaluative methods. Through the test and evaluation of both low and high-fi prototypes, I evaluated my concept idea and defined the final direction for the project.

  • Casual Observation

    To gain an overview of the selected context with all the possible scenarios

  • Desk Research

    To get inspired by other people’s research/papers. To check what is already known about this topic and make connections between different fields

  • Survey & Interview

    To understand people's experiences, needs, thoughts, and feelings through both structured surveys and open, flexible discussions.

  • Roleplay

    To compare people’s typical behavior while walking with the phone and without it in a crowded environment to highlight pain points and possible intervention

  • Ethnography

    To take into account the smartphone point of view

  • Sensitising Activity

    To gain some extra insights into user’s needs through the generative and making technique

Key Insights

There are similarities in the experience of blind people & people that use their phone. The peripherical view gives information about the surrounding environment. The awareness of the world around depends on the commitment required by the task on the phone. The position of the phone is always aligned with the tip toes even when moving in a zigzag manner. When using the phone many aspects and details of the context are missed.

Phone users tend to follow and trust the guide of the person in front of them. When using the phone, the path plotted is inconsistent and unpredictable.

prototype

The prototype, as such, has limits but overall I am satisfied with the result achieved. The core function and interactive live response to user actions made testing with users not only possible but also capable of yielding valuable results. The prototype to make it compressible was made of silicone, and the LED light worked through an interactive electric circuit inserted inside the silicone. The whole shape was then covered with a fabric lining to give the feeling of a finished product

Test, evaluations and final insights

The prototype helped to rebalance the current interaction in which in most cases the phone users are really focused on the screen and lose control and awareness of the surrounding environment. At the same time, the other pedestrians (without the phone) feel frustrated due to the inability of catching other intentions. The concept can be judged successful considering the evaluations’ results and the experiences shared by the participants. In view of this, the qualities of the interaction vision are successfully fulfilled by the design properties.

The active Interaction required by the users, by the device, makes them more aware of what is going on around them and less focused on the phone. The user has the perception of being communicative towards the other pedestrians even without having live feedback from the other pedestrians involved in the interaction. The shape reminds of a video game controller, this makes it perceived as playful and engaging. The device makes the participants more conscious of their movements and the consequence was a more straight gait and a more consistent and steady pace. The product has the expected effect when it is known among the community with shared values and needs. In this sense product, contextualization is fundamental.
